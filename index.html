Clay E Wood

I am Clay wood, currently in my 4th year as Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Geosciences at Pennsylvania State University. I earned a B.S. in Physics from Emory University and worked as a research assistant in a fluid mechanics laboratory. I am advised by Dr. Chris Marone and also co-advised by Dr. Parisa Shokouhi on work regarding understanding the mechanical and hydraulic properties of fractured rock. I am conducting well-controlled experiments in the Penn State Rock Mechanics laboratory where I modulate triaxial stresses and pore pressure of fractured Westerly granite samples and use active source ultrasonic monitoring as a way to understand how fractured subsurface evolves subject to dynamics stresses from earthquakes, hydraulic fracturing, and wastewater disposal. This research is currently supported by funds from the Department of Energy.
